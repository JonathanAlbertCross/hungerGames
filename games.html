The Absurd

The bouncing ball navigation serves as an anti-design, brutalist approach that interprets Camus’ exploration of the absurd. It challenges conventional expectations—much like Camus questions traditional ideas of joy and meaning.

Albert Camus’ The Myth of Sisyphus examines the absurdity of life and humanity’s longing for meaning in an indifferent universe. In this philosophical essay, Camus argues that we must confront and accept the absurd, ultimately choosing to live with defiance and joy despite it.
